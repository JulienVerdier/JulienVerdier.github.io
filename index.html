Who I am

Mostly an immunologist, fascinated by the complexity of the immune system and profoundly enjoying coding, data analysis and data visualisation.

More specifically, I obtained my PhD in immunology in 2011 at Paris 6 university. My PhD was about the role of the Th17 response in inflammatory bowel diseases (IBD). After my PhD, I did a first post-doc in Germany in the European consortium SysmedIBD where I studied the role of NF-κB oscillations in IBD and where I performed a screening of micro-RNAs in feces from IBD patients. After that I came back to France, where I used mass cytometry (see the french version of the Wikipedia article I wrote about this technique) to characterize circulating immune cells in a rare autoimmune disease. I am currently analyzing these data using Python (and I like it).
